        Contigo, much like its translation, exudes the warmth of friendship. The small plates are meant to be shared, the soft lighting and wood invite relaxation, and our amicable waiter had the easygoing manner of the Spanish.

        There's not much going on in this little stretch of neighborhood other than the neighborhood bar next door. Which unfortunately means there's not much to do while you're waiting (unless you're smart and make a reservation in advance, which I highly recommend if you want an actual table or patio seat!) other than catch a basketball game and play some pool in The Peaks Bar.

        Stool seats at the sturdy wooden counter opened first, so that's where we two ended up sitting after a 15 minute wait. Not bad considering we came on a whim! Way before our waiter even came I was already hard at work with my iPhone, snapping pictures of EVERYTHING because the whole setup is so picturesque. The stiff linen napkins, blooming red tulips... You can bet when I discovered the outdoor patio and the charming bathroom that I wished I had taken my phone with me on the short journey. The bathroom isn't one of those sterile, polished places - it feels more like a country cottage with its nature-y paint and wooden pallets under the toilet and sink. Contigo is the kind of place where everything has its place, from the decorative mortar and pestle pieces to the defined role of each cook. There's even someone solely dedicated to wiping completed plates clean before setting them out for the waiters (you can tell I took major advantage of the people watching).

        Okay, onto the meat of the matter:
        - Croquetas: we chose two crispy Jamón Serrano croquetas that immediately jump-started our meal. It's pricy, at $3.50 for one meatball sized croquette, but the creamy-crispy combo is something you really need to try. If ham is not your thing, get a different filling!
        - Papas Braves: no bravery necessary for this saucy dish! Jokes and poor pronunciation aside, these are a Spanish favorite (also called papas bravas). Crispy-skinned potatoes for dipping in both aioli and tangy tomato salsa brava. Think  homefries, but better.
        - Tripe with Chorizo and Chickpeas: I didn't know what to expect, even after seeing it in front of me. But the sauce is a party on your tongue. The tripe makes for an interesting slippery texture (like glass noodles) and you never really know what you've scooped out of the stew until it's in your mouth.
        - Sauteed Greens Catalan style: this is not the way to go if you want bang for your buck. The greens seemed to be large leaves of spinach (or a relative) topped with pine nuts and raisins, with flavor assistance from garlic and lemon. Nothing special, would probably order a salad to get my greens next time.
        - Albondigas: these are also supposedly another Spanish classic, but the gaminess of the lamb was very much present. Texture-wise, I loved how crumbly yet juicy they were, but I think this could have been better.

        The dishes here can be hit or miss, and I think as long as you like the ingredients going into the dish, you're safe. It's up to you to take the $12 bite-sized chance. After seeing the check I think Contigo is worth checking out once or twice for the ambiance, but I'll have to see some menu revamp before coming back.

        I have a love-hate relationship with this place.

        First of all, the good:  wonderful appetizers including locally-sourced sardines and anchovies on toasts.  The prix-fixe menu is totally worth the money.  The "secret cut of pork" is one of the best pork I've ever had.  Amazing grilled squids and yummy churros/chocolate dipping sauce.  Cute space.

        The bad:  horrendous service at times.  Servers seem oblivious and even condescending to our questions & requests.  The last 5 times we went, service was terrible for all 5.  Also, please try and space out the timing for prix fixe courses.  The simplest rule is never to bring another dish out when guests are still working on the previous one.  That's not too much to ask right?

        I and my friends have been coming to Contigo since it opened in 2009 (we even followed Brett Emerson's Flickr before he started building the restaurant).  It's one of my go-to places in The City and I always take out-of-town visitors there, as well as birthdays and other celebrations.  This is why it saddens me to have received such bad service over the past year, which makes me think twice now before deciding to visit again.

        I had a double date here at Contigo and we all had an absolute blast. The food was sensational, the wine bottles were tantalizing, the dessert was enticing, the ambiance was intimate and the service was outstanding.

        Contigo boasts a modern, intimate and cozy vibe that makes it the ideal spot for date nights and chummy dinners. The location right off 24th street in charming Noe Valley doesn't hurt either.

        You simply have to order the chef's tasting menu of 7 courses for $42. It's such a freaking steal and you get the satisfaction of trying all the highlights on the menu.

        Here were our 7 courses:
        1. Ham croqueta: cheesy and crispy fried deliciousness. One of the better croquetas I've had in tapas restaurants.
        2. Sardine and avocado toast with pickled onions and smoked salt: Although I was a bit hesitant about sardines, this was quite possibly my favorite of the night. It was literally bursting with flavor at every bite and the texture combination of the soft avocados on top of the smooth sardine on top of the crispy toast... dear god.
        3. Arros negre (squid ink rice with squid): This was the unanimous table favorite as the squid was so plump and oozing with flavor and the rice was perfectly soaked in yummy squid ink. I'd say this was a close second to the sardine and avocado toast for me.
        4. Pork and lamb meatballs in tomato sauce: So well cooked, meaty and tender.
        5. A salad dish that I can't remember: Nothing noteworthy to write home about. It was just a salad.
        6. Patates braves: So crispy and fried to perfection. One of the best patates braves I've had!
        7. Brussel sprouts with jamon serrano, parsnip, capers and cava butter: So freaking flavorful and yummy! I could eat them all day in the wonderful sauce that the sprouts were covered in.
        8. Trout wrapped with prosciutto: The flavor profile of the fatty prosciutto piece seeping onto the perfectly seared trout with the special sauce on top... I can't even...

        All in all, we were beyond impressed with the intricate detail that went into each course. Clearly, the highlights are the unique flavor profiles and intermingling of ingredients. I highly recommend ordering the chef's tasting menu to get the full vision of the chef and soak in all that Contigo has to offer.

        So on your next yearning for some delicious Spanish food, give Contigo a try - you won't be disappointed!

        Happy Eating, Yelpers!

        GOOD FOOD NEVER FAILS ME!!!
        (4.5 stars)

        Am I in Spain? Yup. Spain, San Francisco.

        - FOOD, delicious
        - PRICES, depends
        - SERVICE, excellent
        - AMBIANCE, indoor/outdoor/counter

        I love tapa restaurants, especially the Spanish influenced ones. This has been on my radar for a long time and I love this place tucked away in the back of Noe Valley. Tuesday evenings are Paella night so expect waves of neighborhood locals in the area.

        HEFER STATUS.  

        OXTAIL MINI-HAMBURGESA with PIMENTO CHIPS ($5) - salsa secreto and the pickled onions. tenderness of oxtail patty was a mini party in my mouth!! It was juicy, flavorful, and at every bite it made you want more. The pimento chips were cute; size of dimes!! They tasted like chips with a slightly different taste.

        SALTED CURED SPANISH ANCHOVY MONTADITO ($2/pc) -  house made mato  cheese and cracked pepper. This was served on a light crispy toasted slice of bread. The house-made mato cheese was really nice, very light in flavor and not sharp like many cheeses and the texture was soft as a tablespoon of warm butter. Two small cured anchovy fillets were crossed on top of the mato with drizzled EVOO and cracked pepper. The saltiness of the anchovy was balanced by the mato cheese. Really really delicious.

        WOOD OVEN ROASTED MONTEREY SARDINE AND AVOCADO TOAST ($3/pc) - pickled onion and smoked salt. The sardines were not fishy at all. It was warm from the wood oven, the toasted bread was topped coated with a spread of avocado, layered with the sardine, then the bright pink pickled onion. The creaminess of the avocado, smoky flavor of the salt brought out the sardine, and the pickled tartness was strangely harmonious on the palette.

        XOCOLATA CALENTA ($5) - cup of thick, rich Barcelona-style Tcho Hot chocolate.
        AMB XURROS ($4) -with crispy fritters fried to order.
        The above two belong together as one dessert. HANDS DOWN DELICIOUS!!! The cup of warm chocolate is so good and rich, it's necessary to dump stuff in it. The amb xurros are mini churros and they are SO good. Crunchy on the outside, soft in the inside, covered with cinnamon and sugar. Don't be shy to pack up the leftover chocolate. I did! Never waste a good thing.

        Will I be back? You don't have to ask me twice.

        The only negative is how expensive it is compared to the portion size. For three people we weren't hungry when we left, but we weren't stuffed either. The wine list they have is pretty great, but a bit overpriced. It's definitely great for a date night but bad if your starving.

        I think next time I go I will do the tasting menu that they have preset. It seem like most people were ordering that.
