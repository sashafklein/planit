    A friend had recommended this place as one of his favorites in Noe so I have been wanting to try it for awhile. I came in a group of 8 for a birthday dinner, and we all thought the food was great and the wine was amazing.

    If you have a party of 8 or more, you are automatically required to have their prix fixe menu of $42/person (which ended up being $73/person pre-tip by the time we added wine, dessert and tax). My one complaint about this is that they don't actually tell you in advance what that menu is going to be, and it changes regularly, plus it doesn't include paella (apparently Tuesdays are paella nights). Luckily, they are super accommodating about allergies, and they sent out different dishes where necessary for my friends who were on gluten-free diets.

    We were seated in the back patio area with heat lamps, and it was really nice back there!

    The menu was excellent. A good variety of food and some standouts:
    Sardine and avocado toast with pickled onion and sea salt - a sardine dish will never be a favorite of mine but surprisingly this was actually pretty good even for people who aren't in to sardines.
    Croqueta de jamon serrano - nicely done
    Melon con Jamon - yummy! Always a good pairing but the melon wasn't too mushy and the parma ham tasted so good.
    Cojondongo - a gazpacho salad - this was delicious! Really fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, frisée, some crispy stuff that added texture, grapes for some added sweetness, and a light vinaigrette.
    Squid a la plancha with shelling beans, padron peppers, chorizo - really nicely cooked. Loved the peppers and aioli.
    Black cod - slightly crisped skin and super moist fish - excellent!
    Albondigas (pork lamb and jamon meatballs) in tomato sherry sauce - never a fan of meatballs so I'm not the best judge, but the sauce was tasty.

    Our server recommended 2012 Bodegas y Viñedos Ponce "PF" Manchuela which was an awesome big red, and luckily the cheapest one on the menu too! ($50/bottle). Everyone loved it and we ended up finishing off a few.

    For dessert, we got the flan but more importantly the TCHO chocolate with churros. Delicious! After finishing the churro, I couldn't stop drinking the chocolate, which has added sea salt.

    In general, I found the service very friendly throughout the booking process when I had to call back several times to add people to the reservation. I would say the service during the meal seemed super slow, and it ended up taking nearly 3 hours, which wasn't ideal on a school night. I'm not sure if it's partly because we were outside in the back. The restaurant was hopping throughout the evening though.

    In any case, we loved our evening and I am looking forward to trying paella night! Well done!

    3 STARS. Everything at Contigo had great execution, but after my dining experience at Coqueta, I realized Contigo was just OK (food-wise).

    My friends and I came here on a Sunday night as part of our monthly food outing. Parking was actually not too bad, surprisingly. We were seated in the outdoor patio with the heated lamps. However, the heated lamps got pretty hot because it was heating our backs the whole time... Anyways, the four of us decided to share family style so we could try a little bit of everything.

    Wood Oven Roasted Local Sardine and Avocado Toast: Really liked the texture of the toast as it added a crunch with the sardine.

    Coca (Flatbread) of Wild Nettles: I really liked this dish because it wasn't too cheesy nor too oily/greasy. I really liked how the crust was crispy. Felt pretty healthy just eating that.

    Patates Braves: I thought this dish was just OK. The potatoes were crispy and fried. However, there was a very good portion of potatoes.

    Charred Asparagus: Pretty interesting since it was served with egg and ham. But overall, the taste was just OK. Nothing too special.

    Seared Local Black Cod: One of my favorite dishes of the night. The black cod was soft and moist.

    Monterey Xipirons (squid) a la Planxa: Squid was pretty chewy and wasn't overcooked. I liked how it was presented over squid ink rice and chorizo. One of my other favorite dishes of the night

    Albondigas: Contained pork and lamb in tomato sauce. I wasn't too big of a fan of the albondigas here.. Didn't really like the taste nor the texture.

    TCHO Hot Chocolate with Churros: Churros were the perfect portion size for dessert. The TCHO hot chocolate was really rich and chocolately, but complemented the churros well. However, after having almost the same dessert at Coqueta, I definitely prefer Coqueta's version of the churros with hot chocolate and mixed berries powder.

    HEFER STATUS.

    OXTAIL MINI-HAMBURGESA with PIMENTO CHIPS ($5) - salsa secreto and the pickled onions. tenderness of oxtail patty was a mini party in my mouth!! It was juicy, flavorful, and at every bite it made you want more. The pimento chips were cute; size of dimes!! They tasted like chips with a slightly different taste.

    SALTED CURED SPANISH ANCHOVY MONTADITO ($2/pc) - house made mato cheese and cracked pepper. This was served on a light crispy toasted slice of bread. The house-made mato cheese was really nice, very light in flavor and not sharp like many cheeses and the texture was soft as a tablespoon of warm butter. Two small cured anchovy fillets were crossed on top of the mato with drizzled EVOO and cracked pepper. The saltiness of the anchovy was balanced by the mato cheese. Really really delicious.

    WOOD OVEN ROASTED MONTEREY SARDINE AND AVOCADO TOAST ($3/pc) - pickled onion and smoked salt. The sardines were not fishy at all. It was warm from the wood oven, the toasted bread was topped coated with a spread of avocado, layered with the sardine, then the bright pink pickled onion. The creaminess of the avocado, smoky flavor of the salt brought out the sardine, and the pickled tartness was strangely harmonious on the palette.

    XOCOLATA CALENTA ($5) - cup of thick, rich Barcelona-style Tcho Hot chocolate.
    AMB XURROS ($4) -with crispy fritters fried to order.
    The above two belong together as one dessert. HANDS DOWN DELICIOUS!!! The cup of warm chocolate is so good and rich, it's necessary to dump stuff in it. The amb xurros are mini churros and they are SO good. Crunchy on the outside, soft in the inside, covered with cinnamon and sugar. Don't be shy to pack up the leftover chocolate. I did! Never waste a good thing.

    Will I be back? You don't have to ask me twice.
