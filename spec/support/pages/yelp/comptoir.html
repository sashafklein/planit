    Listed in Yelp 100 Challenge!

    On the carrefour de L'Odeon, just a few steps from boulevard Saint Germain, stands a restaurant that I have been wanting to eat at for a long time. Ever since Anthony Bourdain extolled its virtues during his Paris episode, Le Comptoir du Relais has been on my bucket list of must-try foodie destinations. Bourdain rarely gets it wrong, and I have to say that the food here did not disappoint.

    But just getting in is half the battle. If you don't have a reservation, the time to come is during lunch. Guests of the accompanying hotel are given priority during dinner, so reservations can be really hard to com by. But if you just want to taste what this restaurant offers, they take in the lunch crowd on a first come, first served basis; a line starts to form about 15 minutes before they open at 11:30.

    I highly recommend the Toast de Foie Gras. It was an object lesson in balance and contrast. The rich slices of foie were perfectly counterbalanced by the salt, pickled onions, and fruit jam. And the toast and accompanying salad provided a nice textural contrast to the creamy and unctuous liver. A great but decadent way to begin the meal.

    The Tartare de Beouf was delicious and unique. I really loved the thought put into this dish. The finely chopped raw beef was made even more rich with the gooey egg, but it was offset by the textural crunch of the croutons and the salsa verde which added some nice acid and flavor. But the use of purple shiso really made the dish. It lifted the beef tartare with its bright, herbal, almost Asian-inspired flavor. The only downside of the dish was that the croutons tasted of rancid oil. But the dish as a whole was really a standout.

    My main complaint, and perhaps the reason I am hesitant to give this place 5 stars, is because the woman who took my order -- and runs the place, it appears -- seemed rather impatient and put out that her restaurant was such a popular place. Texting my order into an iPhone, she never made eye contact while I was ordering, never smiled, and never said thank you. This impertinence was not just for me either; she was this way with the French couple sitting next to me too.

    But that aside, this is really a terrific restaurant with outstanding and creative food. You might want to brush up on your French before coming here though, or use a translator app on your phone, because there is no English menu, and the staff is too harried to translate for you. For when the poor tourist sitting next to me ordered poulpe rôti (roast octopus) thinking it was poulet rôti (roast chicken), she was quite shocked when her dish arrived. She tried returning it (twice!), until she just went ahead and ate it. Turns out she liked it after all. Which goes to show that even when she didn't want it, she still enjoyed it. Yes, the food is that good.

    4 1/2 stars

    I finally got to cross off Le Comptoir du Relais from my Paris bucket list this summer and it was a culinary must. It's like going to China and NOT visiting the Great Wall of China (which I've unfortunately done).

    The first thing you'll notice is that the menu is all in french. I actually had to use my yelp app to look at the pictures and corresponding captions in order to help me order.

    Much to the chagin of our very French waitress, we ordered a lot of food. Every time we would ask for another item, she would shake her head and say "too much" too much."

    I ordered the Toast de Foie Gras and escargot for my entree and both were amazing especially the foie gras which was sliced thin and the sprinkle of salt and pepper made the dish pop for me. The Escargot are your standard fare, but one must order when in Paris!

    We also ordered the vegetable plate of the day which was amazing and split the filet mignon which was the only miss of the night. The meat wasn't as tender as we're used to and had a little too much gristle.

    Our belies stuffed, we wondered around the area, L'Odeon and found a nice cafe across the street to end the evening.

    Careful folks, it's easy to confuse this place with the place next door, L'Avant Comptoir.

    L'Avant Comptoir (Before the Counter) is an appetizer/tapas/wine bar where customers stand while eating. Le Comptoir du Relais (The Counter of Relais) is a full service restaurant where customers sit down among tables and chairs. Both places are located next to each other and both are owned by famed Master Chef TV judge/restauranter Yves Camdeborde.

    I opted for this full service restaurant one Sunday afternoon. After waiting in line for thirty minutes I eventually got seated at a nice table outside on the sidewalk. It was perfect for people watching.

    I didn't experience any bad service like some other folks. However my advice is to have a French translator installed on your phone if you don't know any French. Because if you're like me you might have some trouble interpreting the French menu.

    So I picked items I could understand and asked my server for her best soup recommendation. The lobster bisque she recommended was OK but oddly it didn't contain one piece of lobster. Weird. My sausage platter was OK, but it also was not mind blowing.

    The best part of my meal was probably my chocolate mousse. But at $9 Euros it didn't come cheap.

    One word of advice, don't get the full dinner plates for lunch. I noticed many tourists were ordering full dinner meals for lunch. That's a little too heavy and too expensive for lunch IMO. I recommend doing what I did and just order a soup and small plate for lunch. With wine and bread it can actually be filling.

    I'm not sure if I would return again. The food didn't blow me away, the service was average, and the whole atmosphere felt a little too hectic and rushed. Some of the other brasseries I visited in Paris were more slow paced, relaxing, and of better value. If I did return again, it would be for just a late night dessert and wine.

    So thankful that my friends were staying at the Hotel Relais Saint-Germain during our recent trip to Paris... which I believe made it easy for them to get us a reservation. From what I understand, Le Comptoir is known for long waits, and long lead times for reservations. Thankfully, we had an "in."

    The restaurant is a tiny one, but packed with patrons all taking part in the prix fixe meal. And to be honest, while I loved the meal, I honestly do not remember most of it because of the ridiculous fromage platter that came out before the dessert course of our meal. Because of that platter, I will never look at cheese the same again. Cheese in the U.S. will never be the same... camembert in Paris?! Amaze. As well as every other cheese that was on that plate.

    I swear. Next time I am in Paris, I will have to go back just to experience the fromage again. It was truly life-changing À mon avis. (Probably would never fly in the States, but hey... when in Paris, do as the Parisians do!)
