        1/20/2015

        Star rating is a combination of the venue and food vendor Raj +Singh, where the venue is upping the rating because The Hall is a wonderful little oasis / diamond in the rough. It's a great place to chill and get some daylight without being amongst the TL critters.

        That said, instead of getting Indian food in the heart of the TL today, where great Indian food restaurant choices (with free chai!) abound, I gave Raj + Singh a try. When I entered, The Hall had no lines / queues. Early lunch for me today was at about 11:30.

        So, let me first say that the customer service was 100%. Incredibly friendly and accommodating staff, just, the food...

        If you enjoy mildly flavored / more bland, maybe American friendly Indian, then this is the spot for you. If, however, you prefer all the delicious spices and depth of Indian food flavors, then please read on.

        I sampled a number of choices:
        Mango Lassi
        Chai
        Rose water
        Samosa
        Spinach Curry
        Vegetable Curry
        Chicken Tikka Masala
        Naan

        ...and I was sadly disappointed. Here's the breakdown of each item:

        LASSI: Milky (not creamy/smoothie-like) and light on the mango. Not fresh tasting and made from concentrate (Note to previous writer re: the lassi -- most places pour it from a premade batch, but it can still taste

        CHAI: Too much fennel for my palate (AKA: fake licorice flavor), not enough spiciness

        ROSE WATER: I enjoy myself the occasional rose water beverage, cocktail, what have you (Really, I get rosewater anything at every opportunity). I have a bottle at home for flavoring beverages. This rose water, however, is actually pink and tastes like you're drinking restroom deodorizer or rose-scented cleanser. Already, most people don't like the flower flavored drinks I enjoy that are far lighter in rose flavor. The fact that I don't enjoy this is saying something.

        SAMOSA: Warm (not hot), greasy (probably accentuated by the fact that it wasn't hot)

        CURRIES: Veggie & Chicken are in Tikka Masala sauce. They were equally warm (not hot). They were okay. Mildly spiced due to milk / cream content. No major complaints other than the temperature.

        SPINACH CURRY: Tasted like slightly burnt spinach. Also just warm, even though it was straight out of the catering style tin.

        NAAN: Three dollars for a SINGLE piece. Really? Paid for it. Never received it. My food would have been completely cold by the time it probably would have been done and I received the samosa in lieu of the naan.

        Thank you guys for trying to make the naan fresh, but it should be fresh by the hour or half hour, not per order with the food already being so cold. I'm not waiting that long. Also, samosas should cost more than naan, not the other way around.

        I was really hoping this would be a new, yummy spot for Indian that's not so grody inside (although, Pakwan in the TL is relatively clean and has a nice, open space). I'm sticking to the heart of the TL for Indian in these parts.

        ..::Fine and Rare::..

        The albacore tuna melt on a pretzel bun with side salad is a tangy, delicious dream at a reasonable price point ($8).

        The salad greens were perfectly, lightly dressed in a salty dressing with a sprinkle of pomegranate arils. My favorite part was the side of PINK pickled cauliflower! :D

        The toasted pretzel bun looks tiny but the kitchen stuffs it so well that this is more like a fork sandwich than a hand sandwich. Ya know what I mean? The albacore tuna salad has chunks of tender tuna with tiny crunchy bits of celery (I think). And then there's the melty slice of cheddar cheese. And then some sliced, crisp red onion. Last but not least, a generous dollop of sauerkraut. Mmm!

        The only thing I would have done differently would be to have the cheese melted on the bottom or top bun so the tuna or veggies don't cool down the melted cheese.

        Listed in I'm a CARNIWHORE!, Why SF is a Foodie's Gourmet Paradise

        4.5 STARS

        Mr. NSS: Where do you wanna go for date night tonight?"
        Me: "Let's check out the new food court."

        Mr. NSS: "Food court? Damn girl, you must REALLY want that ring! Where's it at?"
        Me: "Market and 6th."

        Mr. NSS: "Oh near that TI....never mind."
        Me: "You talking about that TITTY bar?" (o_O)

        Mr. NSS: "Ummm yah, but I've only 'heard' of it yanno...but anyways, tell me more about this food court..."

        Opening on Tuesday, September 30th, The Hall is the fanciest thing Market and 6th has seen since...well, it's the fanciest thing that has ever touched those pee pee stained streets. Like a micro mini San Pedro Square (Man Jose), The Hall houses five food vendors, a coffee bar, and a bar (wine, beer, and loophole cocktails).

        All seating is communal and they have a projection screen TV for game nights: bit.ly/1rzxDbO. Since it's Indian summer, definitely take advantage of the outdoor seating: bit.ly/1rGZS5Y. There's only ONE restroom for women though! Ugh.

        The Hall is only a temporary venue (open till 2016). Currently, they're open everyday from 8 am - 8 pm. The bar is open from 11 am to 11 pm. Vendors take CC's via Square.

        Divide and conquer if you want to try everything. It was just Mr. NSS and I so we only tried four of the six vendors.

        ~ * ~ D R I N K S ~ * ~
        *Bar Menu: bit.ly/1rPi3YJ

        Since they don't have hard liquor, I decided to try one of their Loophole Cocktails: Orchard Punch ($8): bit.ly/10mpXhF. It was fantastic and had all the flavors I love: chamomile, apple (hard cider), honey and mint. Two got me buzzed.=)

        I don't drink coffee so didn't try Dignitá but here's their Yelp reviews for their shop in Dogpatch: yelp.com/biz/dignita-san….

        ~ * ~ F O O D ~ * ~
        Here's the menu for the 5 food vendors: thehallsf.com/menu.

        1) THE WHOLE BEAST (A Carniwhore's Delight)

        Their menu looked the most enticing of all the vendors but we only sampled one item since we wanted to try the others.

        **Charcuterie Platter ($14): bit.ly/1E4vetU
        A variety of six meats served alongside house pickled onions, house marinated olives, mustard sauce, baguette, and flatbread. The exact names of all the meats escape me but they were all top-notch, delicious, and perfect for sharing. Loved the mustard sauce which I thought was an mustard aioli (deliciously creamy) but it wasn't.

        The owner was nice enough to come check in with us. He said once that they get their smoker up and running, they will have one smoked meat special a day served in a sammie or a salad.

        2) CASSIA (Moroccan & Peruvian Fusion)

        **Coconut & Chipotle Braised Pork Shoulder w/ quinoa & cabbage slaw ($12): bit.ly/1rzsAZ9

        The best value of the bunch (based on portion sizes I saw compared to other vendors), I really enjoyed Cassia as much as The Whole Beast. The Pork Shoulder was uber tender and the coconut sauce was creamy with a nice kick. Loved that they offer quinoa and brown rice so I can pretend to eat healthy. The cabbage slaw was refreshing with a good balance of crunch and acid.

        3) RAJ + SINGH (Indian curries)

        **Chicken Tikka Masala w/ naan ($9): bit.ly/1vcF0DZ

        The owner(s) were really friendly and bubbly but unfortunately, this was my least favorite vendor of the three food vendors we tried. The chicken was too dry for my liking (white meat) and there wasn't enough sauce (not too spicy) for dipping. Also, the portion size was small for $9.

        4) LITTLE GREEN CYCLO (Vietnamese)

        I tried them once (food truck) and wasn't a fan so we skipped Little Green Cyclo. Here's their yelp reviews for their food truck: yelp.com/biz/little-gree….

        5) FINE & RARE (Seafood)

        I regret not trying Fine & Rare because they had the longest line of all the vendors. Their Smoked Oyster Gumbo was calling my name but my muffin top couldn't take anymore.

        The only vendor The Hall is missing is a dessert one. Ahem, may I suggest Frozen Kuhsterd: yelp.com/biz/frozen-kuhs…?

        All the staff from the various vendors were very friendly and enthusiastic. Someone will take away your glasses and trash once you're done eating/drinking (no trash bins in sight).

        Lots of yipsters and young professionals were at The Hall. It's nice to see one of SF's seediest areas getting gentrified. Yah, it's driving up prices but if it weren't for places like The Hall, I wouldn't trek to Market/6th.

        If you drive there, I advise trying to find parking on 7th or 8th and then walking up. There's plenty of street parking on Mission right at 6 pm. Beware of your surroundings walking to and from your car please!

        As we were walking back to the car, we were in search of the TITTY bar (across The Hall)...

        Me: "Awww man, it's closed for remodeling!"
        Mr. NSS: "You've been there before?"

        Me: "Nothing like some 3 am T & A!" *giggles*
        Mr. NSS: "WTF, were YOU doing there at 3 am?!?!" (o_O)

        Listed in Ed U.'s Tenderloin List, Ed U.'s Unique Cuisine List

        Making the mid-Market area a draw for foodies is no small feat. I pass through this area on the bus every day, and believe me, nobody wants to get off at 6th Street unless they're trying to score some hallucinogenic substance or they're a pack of German tourists looking for Fisherman's Wharf without a map or a clue. But finally, there's a reason to come here for the rest of us, and it's a smart use of space that had remained unoccupied since Hollywood Billiards closed shop over six years ago. It's a communal pop-up, a bit like 331 Cortland in Bernal Heights (where Paulie's Pickling is) only much bigger with tables (photo: bit.ly/1xBiqcN) and an inviting on-site bar which is open until 11PM. The food stands close at 8PM.

        Speaking of which, there are six other businesses in the space which remind me of the food courts of yore when my mom let us decide which greasy international food we could have for lunch when we were at the mall. However, there is a healthier, hipster-centric vibe to the eateries - a Vietnamese spot, an Indian kiosk with vegan options, another stand focused on Southern-influenced seafood, another a deli that uses all parts of the animals. After some perusing, I decided to try Cassia, an interesting hybrid of Moroccan and Peruvian cuisine where for $12, I got a nice serving of the Braised Pork Shoulder over Rice with Napa & Red Cabbage Slaw (photo: bit.ly/1rH6Edp). Although I wish there was more kick to the main dish, it was hearty eating, quite tasty.The only challenge was the server's inability to operate the computer-operated cash register.

        Considering that this was the first day of operation, I was surprised how smoothly things appeared to be running regardless of the glitches. Perhaps because it hasn't been quite discovered yet, there was seating available, but I could easily see it getting packed on weekends with little recourse but to take the food to go. Where you can go in that neighborhood is not that optimal given the number of derelicts lurking outside. Even though it's only a temporary set-up (a year or so), it's a solid idea to repurpose otherwise wasted space like this. In fact, the whole communal concept allows aspiring chefs to show off their skills without bearing the full financial brunt of property costs and maintenance. Now if Mayor Ed Lee could produce a West Coast version of the High Line, then I may have to give him some props.

        This super cool new place in Mid-Market turned out to be a great place to watch a World Series game after work. The setup is a big open area, in a building with kind of a reclaimed/open/rustic vibe, with exposed beams and open ceiling, and old doors making up up one wall. There are booths around the perimeter with different kinds of food. There is a full bar up front, long tables in the middles, and sidewalk seating out front.

        My group got a curry and rice bowl, as well as naan and a samosa from Raj + Singh, and a huge charcuterie platter as well as roasted brussel sprouts from The Whole Beast. The curry and samosa were both pretty tasty, however the naan was cold, a little rubbery, and lacked that distinctive char on the outside. The platter from the Whole Beast left us amazed. There was six types of cured meats, six types of cheeses, some fruit, almonds, olives, pickled veggies, mustard, and two types of bread to hold everything together on its way to your moth. Everything on the plate was absolutely fantastic!

        The happy hour specials are great too - it was only $3 for a pint of beer and $12 for pitchers. I can definitely see myself coming back here for future nights after work!

        Should we eat outside?...Well how safe do you feel outside?
        Its a conversation you might have in the market area but if you are used to this area you know how to make it. The Hall is smack dab in the middle of the foot traffic and shady business of Market street but it has done a lot to help clean up the area which is a welcome change.

        The renovated space won't be here forever but while it is here you owe it to yourself to check it out. Once you enter the doors you are greeted by what I would describe as a mini ferry building. There are different shops with diverse types of foods and all look and smell delicious. There are options for vegetarians as well as carnivores and even a fish shop as well.

        The space is fantastic. There is a 20's type saloon feel with many of the old buildings pool hall aesthetic still in place. It just feels fun and light hearted and doesn't take itself too serious. Its a great change of pace from the stop and go traffic outside and the crazies that can lounge around the area. The place is bright with huge menus and ample seating. The outside seating is nice although you are privy to whoever is out there. The inside has several benches and community tables. I should mention the place is super family friendly despite the fact that alcohol is served there. I went on a Wednesday night and there were several kids running around next to businessmen having a beer after work and bros watching the Giants game. Its kind of surreal but wholly San Francisco.

        The coffee shops and bar in the place are probably going to see the most use and I have to go back and try the food. But the cheap drinks were great and in an area that doesn't have many options for drinks after work this is a nice option while it lasts. Not really a huge venue so don't bring 20 people but its a cozy open area food market with a flair. Check it out before its gone.
