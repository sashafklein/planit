The Essential 38 Portland Restaurants, October '14

Updated by Danielle Centoni on Oct 8 2014, 1:54p

It's time to update the Eater 38, your answer and ours to any question that begins, "Can you recommend a restaurant...?" This highly elite group covers the entire city, spans myriad cuisines, and collectively satisfies all of your restaurant needs, save for those occasions when you absolutely must spend half a paycheck. Every couple of months, we'll be adding pertinent restaurants that were omitted, have newly become eligible (restaurants must be open at least six months), or have stepped up their game.

This time around, after much reflection, we must make room by retiring three stalwarts: Taqueria Y Panaderia Santa Cruz , Nuestra Cocina, and Levant. In their place we welcome Davenport, a neighborhood joint with a refined touch, Kachka, the cozy house of Russian delights, and Taqueria Nueve, Portland's prodigal son.

In a years-long, very close race with Ken's Artisan Pizza, Apizza Scholls stills emerges as the town's best pie. Co-owner/pizzaiolo Brian Spangler shows respect for his dough, refusing to overload his crisp, quick-fired crusts with more toppings than they can handle. Thankfully, there's no need for DIY concoctions given the pizzeria's perfected equation of sauce, fresh mozzarella, pecorino romano, and in the case of the Pizza Amore, salty capicola scattered on top. Incidentally, that's where Scholls still stands, despite the recent influx of solid Portland pizza.

  2. 1 Apizza Scholls
    Pizza Place, $$$$
    Visit website
    (503) 233-1286
    EXPAND CARD

    Portland, OR 97215
    GET DIRECTIONS
    SHOW IN MAP

    In a years-long, very close race with Ken's Artisan Pizza, Apizza Scholls stills emerges as the town's best pie. Co-owner/pizzaiolo Brian Spangler shows respect for his dough, refusing to overload his crisp, quick-fired crusts with more toppings than they can handle. Thankfully, there's no need for DIY concoctions given the pizzeria's perfected equation of sauce, fresh mozzarella, pecorino romano, and in the case of the Pizza Amore, salty capicola scattered on top. Incidentally, that's where Scholls still stands, despite the recent influx of solid Portland pizza.
