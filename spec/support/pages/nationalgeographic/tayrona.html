Tayrona National Park

Tayrona National Park, Santa Marta, Santa Marta (Distrito Turstico Cultural E Histrico), Magdalena, Colombia

Posted by
NatGeoEditor, Staff
October 13, 2011

Flag

Report Inappropriate Content

More »

This is Colombia at its most tropically pristine, with jungle-edged beaches, ancient ruins, and lively howler monkeys. It's named for the Tayrona, one of the most important pre-Columbian peoples in South America, who lived and traded in this area until the end of the 16th century, when Spanish conquerors forced them into the mountains. Swim at Cabo San Juan de Gua (Arrecifes can be dangerous with its sneaky tide), then walk to Pueblito, once a Tayrona village, with impressive terraced dwellingsnot unlike Ciudad Perdida, though much, much smaller.
