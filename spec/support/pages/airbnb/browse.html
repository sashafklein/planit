Description

The Space

The location of the apt is very comfortable for your different needs.
It's about 25 minutes from the airport. 10 minutes from downtown and the old part of the city called "La Candelaria" which is surrounded by museums,galleries,shops,bars and restaurants.
Also in a close range there are different known tourist areas,like "zona T, parque 93 and zona G" also packed with a variety of dinning experiences or places with different music alternatives.

Guest Access

The bedroom has it's own bathroom(shower,sink,toilet),outside of the room,for the guest use only.
Fully equipped kitchen, laundry room with washer and dryer,large living room equipped with sound system,terrace with a barb'Q,Hammocks and an option of outdoor breakfast.

Interaction with Guests

I am very involve with the art industry of the city, visiting different exhibitions weekly and willing to take my guests to see the most important art openings in Bogota and also ready to acomodate citytours or visits to nearby little towns.

Carmen

My friend and I stayed in Jack and Eva's apartment for 2 nights in February 2015. We highly recommend it!

The apartment:

It is as nice as it looks in the photos. It is clean, bright and airy, and filled with beautiful artwork, plants and furniture (many of which were made by Eva or Jack). The wrap around terrace offers nice views, and we enjoyed chilling in the hammock watching the sunset! Our room itself was big, and the bed was comfortable. The neighborhood is safe and in a good location (easy access to the tourist areas in La Candelaria and the nightlife areas in Zona T, Parque 93 etc).

The hosts:

Jack was the most gracious host (unfortunately, we did not get to meet Eva because she was busy preparing for her art exhibition). He was very friendly and was always available to answer our questions, but he also gave us our own space when we wanted it. On the day we arrived, he took us to a local restaurant so we could try some traditional food for lunch, and we took a stroll around the neighborhood and he showed us the local markets and introduced us to different local fruits. Every morning, he prepared a great breakfast spread of local breads, cheese and fruits and plentiful coffee. He gave us thoughtful suggestions on how to spend our time in Bogota, each with detailed travel directions. On top of all that, Jack is also an interesting and easy guy to talk to, and we had many good conversations with him and felt that we learnt a lot more about Bogota and Colombia than we would have otherwise.

In short, stay here!
